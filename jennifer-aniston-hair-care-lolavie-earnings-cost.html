Jennifer Aniston's Hair Care Line LolaVie May Earn Her A Fortune Despite Affordable Product Prices

Posted by Billy Koelling on Sunday, August 4, 2024

Highlights

  • Jennifer Aniston's affordable LolaVie hair products are gaining popularity and could boost her $320 million net worth.
  • Aniston is hands-on with her hair care line, creating products with her vision for clean, high-performing results.
  • Collaborating with her hairstylist, Aniston's final product, the Sculpting Paste, is a crucial step in any hair routine.

It's no secret that many celebrities like to start brands and companies so they have other sources of revenue. Selena Gomez runs her unique brand Rare Beauty in addition to her work in the entertainment industry. But not every star makes a ton of money from their product line. For example, fans are curious if Kylie Jenner is having money problems because of Kylie Beauty.

While Friends star Jennifer Aniston's hair line might not seem super different at first glance, it's more affordable than many, which makes it stand out. According to CNN, Aniston created a new product called The Sculpting Paste for her hair line LolaVie, and it came out in April 2024. While Aniston's products aren't as pricey as fans might expect, she still could make good money from selling this product.

In this article, we'll take a look at how Aniston's hair care line might add to her net worth, along with some background on when and why she started the company.

Jennifer Aniston Could Add To Her Net Worth Thanks To Her Hair Care Line LolaVie

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Jennifer Aniston has a $320 million net worth, and it seems likely that she will grow her net worth from her hair care company LolaVie. The products have become increasingly popular over the past few years, and Aniston's fanbase was clearly eager to buy whatever the star was selling.

It's interesting to note that Aniston's LolaVie products are super affordable. While fans might think that they would each cost at least $100, that's not the case at all. The Sculpting Paste, for example, only costs $29.

While it's unknown what Jennifer Aniston earns from her hair care line, she could be bringing in some big bucks. According to Forbes, Rihanna became a billionaire because of her Fenty Beauty line. Forbes also noted that in 2018, Fenty Beauty's profits totaled $570 million. It's also unclear how much money LolaVie makes, but Aniston has called the brand successful in many interviews. The low price of each product is definitely attractive since that makes it easy for fans to buy the products time and time again instead of feeling like they are splurging or have to save up for months.

In 2022, Jennifer Aniston told Forbes that customers have been really happy with LolaVie, which proves that she must be making money from it. She sounded thrilled about how the line has been doing.

She said:

“Well, we’ve had such an incredible reaction from our consumers, so that’s probably the most satisfying. We won eight beauty awards and we had multiple five-star reviews and it just feels that the work we put into this product and the time we took, all of these products, has paid off. We have a lot of return consumers that keep coming back for more."

In her interview with CNN, the actress explained that she and Chris McMillan, who is her hair stylist, created the affordable product Sculpting Paste together. Since McMillan has been talking about the star's hair for decades now, he was definitely the perfect person to collaborate with.

Inside Jennifer Aniston's Hair Care Line LolaVie

Jennifer Aniston started LolaVie in 2021 along with her co-founders Amy Sachs and Joel Ronkin. In her interview with Forbes, Aniston shared that she was interested in being very hands-on with her hair care line. While some celebrities are considered the founders of product lines or companies but let other people make creative decisions, she didn't want to do that.

Aniston said:

“I didn’t want to just slap my name onto something. This has been a really fun and exciting experience for me and something I love to do. My vision, our vision, to create this platform where we could deliver clean, plant-based products that were also high-performing."
