Blaque

Shamari’s singing career began when she was discovered by American rapper Lisa Lopes, who mentored Blaque. She formed Blaque with Brandi Williams and Natina Reed. The group made their first appearance in 1997, in the music video for Lil’ Kim’s “Not Tonight”. Their debut album Blaque sold more than 1.5 million copies and was certified Platinum. Billboard named Blaque as the fourth “Best New Artist” in 1999.

Apart from singing, she has portrayed a cheerleader named Lava in the comedy film Bring It On in 2000. Blaque went on hiatus until 2007 and eventually split in 2008. Natina Reed died after being hit by a car in 2012. Shamari still performs with fellow group member Brandi Williams.

The Real Housewives of Atlanta

In November 2018, DeVoe joined the cast of the Bravo reality television series The Real Housewives of Atlanta for the show’s eleventh season. On September 26, 2019, it was announced that Shamari would not be returning for the twelfth season.

She is working on a collaboration with her husband Ronnie DeVoe called Me & Mari. The duo released a single called “Love Comes Through” on December 7, 2018.
