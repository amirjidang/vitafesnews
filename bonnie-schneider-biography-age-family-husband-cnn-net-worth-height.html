Bonnie Schneider Biography

Bonnie Schneider is a meteorologist on television who authors Extreme Weather and appears on The Weather Channel. She previously worked for Headline News, and Bloomberg Television. 

While working at Bloomberg, she covered Hurricane Sandy for Bloomberg Television from their world headquarters in New York City.

Bonnie Schneider Marriage | Husband

She is married to Michael Samuel Sikowitz, a writer and producer and has undertaken many projects like ‘Friends’, ‘Welcome to the Family’, ‘Grounded For Life’. The couple met in October 1998 for a business lunch after being introduced by a mutual friend. Mr. Sikowitz proposed in July 1999. The couple wedded on April 30, 2000,  by Rabbi Norman Kahan at the Pierre in New York.

The couple spent the morning of their wedding in an unusual way. They invited their guests to join them at the annex of Central Synagogue to participate in a bone marrow screening for Daniel Brown, who has leukemia.

Bonnie Schneider Education

Bonnie studied at Boston University where she earned a bachelor’s degree in journalism. For her meteorological studies, she chose the State University of New York as well as the Hunter College. After completing studies, her journalism career started as a weekend meteorologist at the KPLC-TV situated in Lake Charles, Louisiana.

Bonnie Schneider KPLC-TV | CNN TV

She started her career as an anchor in Lake Charles working for “KPLC-TV” in 1990. However, she was living in Lake Charles, Louisiana where she had the opportunity of being the meteorologist of the weekend. She also worked there as a reporter where she focused on different stories about the lifestyle and entertainment of people from the United States.

After a couple of months working there, she appeared on “CNN TV” in 2005 in the days of the Atlantic hurricane season. This was one of the most dangerous hurricane seasons of all time. She was a really brave woman when she did not have problems with accepting the job of covering all the news about the hurricane. Not only that but she covered the international news of “CNN” when the Cyclone Nargis in Myanmar and earthquakes in China.

Since she loved Journalism and meteorology, she also worked in the TV show called “Last House Standing” which she used to give tips about how to survive bad weather, earthquakes, hurricanes, storms. Because of all her experience with natural disasters, she was known as “The extreme Weather Expert”.
