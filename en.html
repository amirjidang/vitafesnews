Early Life

Ned Arnel Mencia, known professionally as “Carlos Mencia,” was born on October 22, 1967, in San Pedro Sula, Honduras, to his parents Roberto Holness and Magdelena Mencía. He grew up in East Los Angeles, California.

Mencia completed his schooling at Garfield High School located in Los Angeles County and attended the California State University, Los Angeles, but dropped out for a career in comedy.

Personal life

Career

Initially, Carlos Mencia was known as “Ned Holness” on the advice of The Comedy Store’s owner Mitzi Shore. He then went on to perform as a stand-up comedian in various venues like The L. A. Cabaret and The Comedy Store. This led him to appear in ‘Buscando Estrellas’ and ‘The Arsenio Hall Show.’

In 1994, he was selected as the host of ‘Loco Slam’ on HBO. Prior to that, he has guest-appeared in an episode of ‘In Living Color’ in 1990. He was the host of ‘Mind of Mencia’ from 2005 to 2008. He has also hosted shows like ‘Uncensored Comedy: That’s Not Funny,’ ‘Funny is Funny!’, ‘Latino Laugh Festival,’ and has appeared in ‘Comic Relief.’

In 1999, he was seen in ‘Moesha.’ From 2001 to 2005, he lent his voice in ‘The Proud Family.’ Mencia has appeared in various films and television shows like ‘The Shield,’ ‘Outta Time,’ ’29 Palms’, ‘Farce of the Penguins’, ‘The Heartbreak Kid,’ ‘MADtv,’ and ‘Carlos Mencia: New Territory.’

Carlos Mencia has released many albums like ‘Take a Joke America,’ ‘America Rules,’ ‘Unmerciful,’ and ‘Spanglish.’ He has appeared on radio shows for CBS Radio and XM Satellite Radio.

Awards & Achievements
