Highlights

  • Anne Hathaway's career includes successful roles in films like The Devil Wears Prada and Les Misérables, earning her an Academy Award.
  • Hathaway's highest-grossing films include The Dark Knight Rises and Alice in Wonderland, with total worldwide earnings exceeding $6.89 billion.
  • Anne Hathaway has established herself as a versatile actress known for delivering impactful performances and commanding high salaries.

Anne Hathaway has established herself as one of Hollywood's most successful actresses in her distinguished career.

Rising to fame as a teenager with her breakout role in the Disney comedy The Princess Diaries in 2001, she quickly transitioned to adult roles, establishing her range in films like Brokeback Mountain and The Devil Wears Prada, which became her biggest commercial success at that time.

In 2012, she starred as Catwoman in The Dark Knight Rises, her highest-grossing film to date, and delivered a poignant performance as Fantine in Les Misérables, earning her an Academy Award for "Best Supporting Actress."

Hathaway has starred in over 20 films that have collectively grossed over $6.89 billion worldwide. She was among the world's highest-paid actresses in 2015, earning $15 million from her roles in Rio and Interstellar.

Anne Hathaway currently has an estimated net worth of $80 million, and these are the highest-grossing Anne Hathaway movies to date.

10 Get Smart (2008)

Global Box Office, Get Smart - $230,685,453

Pairing with Steve Carell, Hathaway played Agent 99 in this action-comedy adaptation of the classic spy series.

Hathaway brought calm confidence and suave capability to the role, perfectly contrasting Carell's well-meaning incompetence.

While Carell delivered the trademark slapstick humor, Hathaway balanced the action with a touch of class, highlighting her own potential as a leading action star.

In its opening weekend, the film grossed $38.6 million in the United States and Canada, ranking in the top spot at the box office.

Get Smart would go on to earn $130.3 million in North America and $100.3 million internationally, bringing its worldwide total to $230.7 million.

9 Alice Through The Looking Glass (2016)

Global Box Office, Alice Through The Looking Glass - $299,820,798

Following the billion-dollar success of Alice In Wonderland in 2010, Alice Through The Looking Glass failed to capture the same magic in 2016.

Hathaway reprised her role as the ethereal White Queen, this time on a quest to travel back in time and save the Mad Hatter (Johnny Depp).

Hathaway's role was slightly reduced compared to the first film, but her scenes with Helena Bonham Carter's fiery Red Queen provided a spark thanks to their contrasting acting styles.

However, the film's overall narrative weaknesses overshadowed even these strong performances.

Alice Through The Looking Glass struggled to replicate the box office success of the original, grossing $299.5 million worldwide against a production budget of $170 million.

The Hollywood Reporter estimated the film lost the studio around $70 million when factoring together all expenses and revenues.

8 Ocean's Eight (2018)

Global Box Office, Ocean's Eight - $297,795,726

Anne Hathaway joined an all-star ensemble cast in this stylish spin-off of the beloved Ocean's franchise.

Playing self-absorbed actress Daphne Kluger, Hathaway delivered a scene-stealing performance.

Initially appearing as a clueless and insecure celebrity, Daphne becomes the perfect pawn for the elaborate Met Gala heist masterminded by Sandra Bullock's Debbie Ocean.

However, Hathaway infused the character with unexpected layers of resourcefulness and cunning, making it one of her most comedic turns.

Ocean's Eight grossed $140.2 million in the United States and Canada and $157.5 million in other territories, for a total worldwide gross of $297.7 million, against a production budget of $70 million.

7 The Devil Wears Prada (2006)

Global Box Office, The Devil Wears Prada - $326,714,019

The Devil Wears Prada is simply one of Anne Hathaway's most memorable roles.

She played Andy Sachs, a fresh-faced aspiring journalist who lands a coveted position as assistant to the ruthless and demanding fashion magazine editor Miranda Priestly (Meryl Streep, portraying a thinly-veiled version of notoriously persnickety Vogue editor Anna Wintour).

Hathaway holds her own alongside the legendary Streep, delivering a blend of earnestness, humor, and surprising resilience.

Anne Hathaway was reportedly paid $1 million for her role in The Devil Wears Prada, making it the first time she earned a six-figure salary in her career.

The film grossed over $326 million worldwide, against its $41 million budget.

6 Les Misérables (2012)

Global Box Office, Les Misérables - $442,749,679

Tom Hooper's ambitious adaptation of the stage musical Les Misérables featured a star-studded cast.

However, it was Anne Hathaway's devastating performance as Fantine that stole audiences' hearts and earned critics' praise.

The film follows the story of Jean Valjean (Hugh Jackman) and his relentless pursuit of Inspector Javert (Russell Crowe).

Though in a supporting role with limited screen time, Hathaway infused it with such intense emotion that she earned several accolades, including an Academy Award for "Best Supporting Actress."

Hathaway earned $10 million for her role in Les Misérables, marking the first time she received a seven-figure paycheck in her career.

Les Misérables grossed $149.2 million in North America and $293.4 million in other territories, for a total of $442.7 million worldwide.

5 Rio (2011)

Global Box Office, Rio - $483,866,772

In Rio, Anne Hathaway voiced the spirited macaw Jewel, a free-spirited and independent Spix macaw who becomes the unlikely love interest of the domestically raised Blu (voiced by Jesse Eisenberg).

Hathaway's voice performance brought warmth and charm to Jewel, creating a lovable and strong-willed counterpart to Eisenberg's awkward Blu.

The film was a visual feast, bursting with colorful depictions of Rio and its famed carnival atmosphere. The film received generally positive reviews from film critics, who praised the voice acting, characters, and music.

Rio scored $143.6 million in North America, with $341 million from international markets, resulting in a total worldwide gross of $484.6 million.

4 Rio 2 (2014)

Global Box Office, Rio 2 - $498,781,117

From city streets to the Amazonian jungle, Anne Hathaway's Jewel embarked on a new adventure in Rio 2.

The Academy Award winner returned as Jewel in the animated sequel; this time, the adventure takes Blu, Jewel, and their family deep into the Amazon rainforest.

As they try to adapt to their new surroundings, they face threats from an old enemy and encounter a tribe of fiercely independent Spix macaws.

Hathaway's voice performance again captured Jewel's strength and adventurous spirit, adding a layer of maternal warmth as her character navigates raising a family in unfamiliar territory.

Rio 2 built on the success of its predecessor, delivering catchy musical numbers and memorable characters, leading to even greater box office returns.

Rio 2 grossed $131.5 million in North America and $367.2 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $498.8 million, surpassing its predecessor.

3 Interstellar (2014)

Global Box Office, Interstellar - $733,202,212

Christopher Nolan's perception-altering space odyssey, Interstellar, helped launch Hathaway's career into the global spotlight.

Shedding her usual charm for a steely resolve, Hathaway played Dr. Amelia Brand, a resourceful and intelligent scientist leading a mission to find a new home for humanity among the stars.

Interstellar has become one of Nolan's most revered films, known for its use of practical effects and thought-provoking themes.

The film grossed $188 million in North America and $493 million in other regions, resulting in a global total of $681 million during its original release.

With a production budget of $165 million, Interstellar proved to be a profitable venture, with Deadline Hollywood calculating a net profit of $47 million after accounting for production budgets, marketing, and box office grosses.

2 Alice In Wonderland (2010)

Global Box Office, Alice In Wonderland - $1,025,468,216

Down the rabbit hole and into a world of whimsical madness tumbled Anne Hathaway in Tim Burton's 2010 fantasy film, Alice In Wonderland.

The film follows Alice (Mia Wasikowska), now a teenager, as she returns to the magical world of Wonderland to save it from turmoil.

Under the direction of Tim Burton, the film boasts his signature macabre style and unique art direction, creating an immersive world unlike any other.

Hathaway's portrayal of the benevolent White Queen delighted audiences as she battled for control of Wonderland against her tyrannical sister, the Red Queen (Helena Bonham Carter).

Alice In Wonderland grossed $334.1 million in North America and $691.2 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $1.025 billion against a budget of $200 million.

1 The Dark Knight Rises (2012)

Global Box Office, The Dark Knight Rises - $1,085,289,416

Capping off Christopher Nolan's Batman trilogy, The Dark Knight Rises saw Anne Hathaway take on the role of Selina Kyle, better known as Catwoman.

Hathaway brought a fresh take to the character, a skilled thief who could hold her own in a fight and leave audiences guessing her true allegiances.

The character plays a crucial role in the narrative, providing both assistance and challenges for Batman as he confronts his greatest adversary yet.

Her salary for her role as Catwoman in The Dark Knight Rises was $7.5 million, and it is the highest-grossing of Anne Hathaway's movies to date.

Backed by a production budget of approximately $250 million, The Dark Knight Rises grossed over $1 billion worldwide, making it the highest-grossing Batman film to date.
