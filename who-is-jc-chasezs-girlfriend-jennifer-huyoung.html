Who Is JC Chasezs Girlfriend, Jennifer HuYoung?

Posted by Kelle Repass on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

Highlights

  • NSYNC's JC Chasez has been in a long-term relationship with Jennifer HuYoung since 2018, showing strong love.
  • NSYNC's success was marred by a legal battle with their manager and record label, showing the darker side of the music industry.
  • Boyband member Chris Kirkpatrick orchestrated the formation of NSYNC with other members and faced legal issues with manager Lou Pearlman.

JC Chasez started on The Mickey Mouse Club, just like Britney Spears and her ex, Justin Timberlake. JC had a versatile voice that could float into falsettos or belt into powerhouse runs, and from an early age, it was clear that he would pursue a career in music.

As a member of iconic boy band NSYNC, JC was signed to Jive Records. The Y2K pop era, in which the band was big, was a very distinctive time in popular culture: From oversized clothing to choreographed dance moves to futuristic album designs.

NSYNC was formed in 1997 by businessman Lou Pearlman, and they became one of the most successful and recognizable pop acts of the time. Chris Kirkpatrick, Lance Bass, JC Chasez, Joey Fatone, and Justin Timberlake all had the looks and personalities to enter the teen market and dominate it.

But what did their lives look like when they weren't performing? Were they in relationships? Were they married? Today we're going to take a look at member JC Chasez's personal life, and dive into his relationship with Jennifer HuYoung.

Who Is JC Chasez's Girlfriend, Jennifer HuYoung?

Jennifer and JC have been dating since 2018. She is an executive assistant at Broad and Cassel LLP.

According to LinkedIn, Jennifer HuYoung has a proven track record for keeping the busy schedules of company leaders under control while helping to manage the day-to-day operations of a bustling office.

There's no doubt of the strong love between these two. As proof of it, JC Chasez used This I Promise You lyrics to profess his love for Jennifer on social media.

JC And Jennifer HuYoung Are Rather Private

Although JC Chasez was once one of the most talked about pop stars in the world back when NSYNC was all the hype, the artist chooses to live a rather low-key life nowadays. Not much is known about the details of his relationship with Jennifer and if the two ever plan on tying the knot.

The rare glimpse into their lives comes from the Instagram photos JC Chasez posts with Jennifer every so often.

NSYNC Was Releasing Hit After Hit Back In The Day

It wouldn't be until their second studio record, titled No Strings Attached, that NSYNC genuinely defined themselves as a force to be reckoned with. The forward-thinking production and ahead-of-its-time writing led to groundbreaking success.

Ironically, when they were the most successful, they became entrenched in a messy legal battle with their manager and record label. Fighting for their name, NSYNC would have to overcome bad contracts and greedy men trying to take advantage of them.

Finding The Perfect Band Members

Member Chris Kirkpatrick started the creation of the boy band. Chris was in college working part-time in a doo-wop group at Universal Studios in Orlando, Florida. While working there, he was connected through a mutual friend to a man named Lou Pearlman.

One day, Lou carted a private plane to the group New Kids On The Block. After asking them what they did and observing how much money they made in record sales, he decided to create boy bands. After completing the Backstreet Boys, Lou told Chris to find another boy band to fund them.

Chris started making some calls and found Justin Timberlake, who had previously worked on The Mickey Mouse Club. Justin agreed and referred his friend and co-star, JC Chasez, to be a part of the group.

NSYNC's Legal Issues

In November 1999, NSYNC launched a countersuit against Lou Pearlman for 25 million dollars, citing fraud.

In court documents, JC Chasez called Pearlman "an unscrupulous, greedy businessman who while hugging us and calling us' family' was picking out pockets, robbing us of our future and even endangering our health."

Tax forms filed on behalf of NSYNC indicated that members of the group earned only approximately 20,000 dollars each in 1997 and 38,000 dollars in 1998. Despite this, their record label and management were making hundreds of millions of dollars in revenue.

The court battle became a highly publicized event, with NSYNC fans praying for the win outside the courtrooms. On November 24, 1999, Judge Anne C. Conway denied BMG's and Lou's attempt to stop the group from using their name and releasing their second studio album until after the case went to trial.

Luckily, before the case had to go to trial, on December 23, 1999, it was announced that NSYNC, Transcontinental, and Jive Records could come to a compromise and settle out of court.
