    Cortney Hall Career

    Hall previously worked as an integral part of the top-rated morning news in Chicago, delivering the headlines in the 9 o’clock hour of the WGN Channel 9 Morning News and also reporting trending news. She consistently turned highlight bundles, one of which sent her all the way to Senegal to follow her tribal roots.

    However, after seven years of service, Hall decided to take a starring role of her own. On Wednesday, August 21, 2019, was her last day at the network to head to NBC’s owned WMAQ-Channel 5 to co-host a new lifestyle show in development.

    “After careful consideration, I have decided to take my career in an exciting new direction. It has been a privilege to work here over the past seven years. WGN has given me opportunities to turn stories that I am proud of, anchor alongside the best in the business, and to grow as a morning news personality and journalist,” Hall told colleagues in a memo.

    Hall’s surprise departure was the second in two days at WGN. Amy Rutledge, the traffic and feature reporter who doubled as a fill-in news anchor, left, sources said, to join Weigel Broadcasting WCIU-Channel 26 as co-host of “The Jam,” the weekend morning talk and entertainment show.

    Prior to joining WGN News team, Cortney Hall worked as a news anchor and reporter at WKMG-TV in Orlando, Florida. Her career started at Bloomberg News in New York City, where she worked as a producer, making various morning news segments. In addition to her career, she reported at WCIA, the CBS affiliate in Champaign, Illinois.
