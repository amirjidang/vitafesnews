Welcome to the fascinating history of Indeed, the world’s leading job search platform. In this article, we will take a closer look at the origins of Indeed, its founders, the journey of the company, and its impact on the job market. Join us as we explore the beginnings of Indeed and how it has revolutionized the way job seekers find employment opportunities.

Key Takeaways:

  • Indeed was founded in November 2004 by Paul Forster and Rony Kahan.
  • It is currently a subsidiary of Recruit Holdings, a Japanese company.

  • The company operates on a pay-per-click business model.
  • Indeed has become the world’s number one job site, attracting millions of job seekers.

The Founders of Indeed

Indeed, the renowned employment website, was co-founded by Paul Forster and Rony Kahan in 2004. With a shared vision, Forster and Kahan recognized the need for a job search platform that specifically catered to finance professionals. Their entrepreneurial journey began in 1998 when they launched their first venture, Jobsinthemoney.com.

This initial endeavor proved successful, and after the sale of their first company, Forster and Kahan set out to revolutionize the job search industry. It was their ambition to create the world’s first search engine for jobs, providing job seekers with a comprehensive and user-friendly platform. Thus, Indeed.com was born, marking the beginning of their remarkable story.

Driven by their passion for helping people find meaningful employment, Forster and Kahan paved the way for a new era in job search technology. Their innovative approach and dedication to their mission have propelled Indeed to become a global leader in the industry.

“We wanted to create a platform that would empower job seekers and simplify the job search process. Our goal was to connect talented individuals with the right opportunities, helping them thrive in their careers.” – Paul Forster

Today, Indeed stands as a testament to the vision and determination of its founders. Their pioneering spirit continues to drive the company forward, as it evolves to meet the ever-changing needs of job seekers and employers alike. The founding story of Indeed serves as an inspiration for entrepreneurs and job seekers around the world, showcasing the power of innovation and the impact it can have on transforming industries.

Forster and Kahan’s journey with Indeed has spanned over a decade, marked by continuous growth, global expansion, and a commitment to excellence. Their unwavering dedication has not only revolutionized the job search landscape but has also enriched the lives of countless individuals by connecting them with opportunities that align with their skills and aspirations.

The Business Model of Indeed

Indeed operates on a pay-per-click business model, similar to Google’s advertising model. The site indexes job content from various sources, including job boards, recruitment agency sites, and employer’s recruitment pages, and offers companies the option to pay a fee for higher visibility in search results. This model has allowed Indeed to generate revenue and become one of the most profitable job search platforms in the world.

Companies can choose to sponsor their job listings on Indeed, giving them greater exposure to job seekers. When job seekers use Indeed’s search engine to find relevant positions, sponsored job listings appear at the top of the search results, increasing their visibility and attracting more applicants. Employers only pay when job seekers click on their sponsored listings, ensuring that their investment is cost-effective and based on actual engagement with their job postings.

Indeed’s pay-per-click model benefits both employers and job seekers. Employers can effectively target their desired audience and receive more qualified applications, while job seekers have access to a wide range of job opportunities from reputable companies.

By offering companies the opportunity to increase the visibility of their job listings, Indeed has become the go-to platform for employers seeking qualified candidates. The success of this business model has contributed to the rapid growth and popularity of Indeed, solidifying its position as a leader in the job search industry.

The Benefits of the Pay-Per-Click Model:

  • Targeted advertising to relevant job seekers

Global Expansion

After establishing itself as a successful job search platform in the United States, Indeed embarked on a journey of global expansion. Recognizing the growing demand for a reliable and efficient job search platform worldwide, Indeed set out to bring its services to job seekers and employers around the globe.

Over a span of six years, Indeed made significant strides in expanding its operations and reach. The company translated its website into 26 different languages, ensuring that job seekers from diverse backgrounds and regions could access its services in their preferred language.

Indeed’s commitment to global expansion led to the establishment of sales offices in key markets. Today, the company has offices in the UK, Ireland, US, Canada, and Japan, among others. This widespread presence allows Indeed to connect job seekers with employment opportunities on a global scale.

Thanks to its dedication to expanding its reach, Indeed now attracts over 100 million jobseekers every month. This impressive figure is a testament to the platform’s popularity and effectiveness in helping individuals find meaningful employment.

Acquisition by Recruit Co, Ltd.

In September 2012, Indeed took a major step in its journey when it was acquired by Recruit Co, Ltd., a reputable Japanese HR and recruitment services provider. The acquisition, valued at approximately $1 billion, marked a significant milestone in the history of Indeed, solidifying its position as a global leader in the job search industry.

This strategic move brought together the strengths and expertise of both companies, enabling Indeed to further expand its reach and impact in the worldwide job market. With the support of Recruit Co, Ltd., Indeed continued to innovate and enhance its platform, delivering exceptional value to both job seekers and employers.

“The acquisition by Recruit Co, Ltd. was a game-changer for Indeed. It opened up new opportunities and resources that allowed us to accelerate our growth and make an even greater impact in the job search industry.”

Through this acquisition, Indeed further solidified its commitment to connecting job seekers with meaningful employment opportunities and providing businesses with the tools they need to recruit top talent effectively.

The Indeed Logo and Branding

The Indeed logo, unveiled in 2005, features a simple lowercase wordmark that portrays the company’s strong commitment to helping job seekers find opportunities. The standout element of the logo is the stylized letter “i” with a curved arc above it, resembling an eye or an abstract human figure. This unique design captures the essence of Indeed’s mission to provide a reliable platform for individuals in their job search journey.

The use of blue as the predominant color in the logo symbolizes trust, safety, and peace of mind. It evokes a sense of professionalism and reliability that job seekers can rely on when utilizing Indeed’s services. The combination of the lowercase letters and the distinctive “i” mark creates a visually appealing and recognizable logo.

Indeed’s branding is rooted in its history and the background of its founders. By understanding the needs of job seekers and building a platform that connects them with potential employers, Indeed has established itself as a trustworthy and respected brand in the job search industry.

Protection of User Information

At Indeed, the protection of user information is of utmost importance. We understand the importance of privacy and take all necessary measures to ensure that your personal data remains secure. We provide job seekers with the option to control the visibility of their profiles, allowing you to set them as private or public according to your preference.

When you set your profile to private, your contact information is kept confidential, safeguarding your privacy and preventing any unauthorized access. Only the companies to which you apply will have access to your sensitive information.

We also take additional steps to protect your personal data. Indeed removes sensitive information, such as addresses, from public profiles, ensuring that your private details are not visible to anyone browsing the site.

In addition to these measures, we offer users the ability to permanently delete their Indeed accounts if they choose to do so. This gives you complete control over your personal information and allows you to remove it from our platform whenever you wish.

With Indeed, you can trust that we prioritize your privacy and are committed to keeping your information safe and secure.

Pricing and Revenue Model

Indeed offers both free and sponsored options for employers to post job listings. The cost per click in the sponsored option ranges from $0.10 to $5, allowing employers to control their budget and the amount they are willing to pay for each click.

Indeed generates revenue through its pay-per-click model, advertising space sales, and other innovative offerings for employers on the platform. Employers can choose to promote their job listings by paying for sponsored visibility, ensuring that their postings reach a wider audience and receive increased visibility in search results.

The pay-per-click model enables employers to optimize their recruitment strategies, as they only pay when a job seeker clicks on their sponsored listing. This cost-effective approach allows businesses of all sizes to leverage the power of Indeed’s platform to attract qualified candidates.
