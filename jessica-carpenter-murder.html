Jessica Carpenter Murder Her Father Sued Airborne Express Inc

Posted by Patria Henriques on Wednesday, August 21, 2024

This article contains details about Jessica Carpenter’s murder. People were searching on the internet to find out about Jessica Carpenter’s murder story. 

Let’s find out how Jessica Carpenter was murdered.

Jessica Carpenter murder

According to IMDB, Jessica was a 17-year-old, and she was murdered in Aiken, South Carolina, on August 4, 2000. Later, Robert F Atkins was arrested for her murder. 

It was found that he entered Jessica’s home dressed in his Airborne Express uniform. Charlie Carpenter, Jessica’s father, sued the Airborne company for neglecting that they hired a person with a criminal background. 

Jessica’s mother, Judy Carpenter, honors the death anniversary of her daughter every year. She places a memorial piece in the Aiken Standard newspaper for her daughter’s memory.

Jessica was the youngest among her three sisters. She worked as a hostess at the local Red Lobster and played for the school band. 

According to the sources, Jessica’s cause of death was a lack of oxygen and internal bleeding. The police have been investigating her murder case for over two years. 

After investigating for two years, Robert Franklin Atkins was arrested for Jessica Carpenter’s murder. After the police interrogated Franklin, they said he was working as a delivery driver for Airborne Express. 

While making deliveries, he was able to meet Jessica Carpenter before murdering her. In 2003, Carpenter’s father, Charlie Carpenter, sued the Airborne Express company.

Franklin saw Jessica when he was delivering a package to her home a few days before the murder, according to the lawsuit. The lawsuit claimed that the company didn’t perform a background check on Atkins and endangered the victims. 

Before getting hired by the company, his record included felony burglary, escape, and weapons charges. While Atkins was serving prison time for second-degree murder, the police confirmed his involvement in Jessica’s murder. 

Police shared a statement in which they said that the samples retrieved from Jessica’s murder spot matched Atkins’s DNA. 

While in prison, the investigators interviewed 300 people and sent more than 100 DNA samples to the state database. After several sample checks, Atkins’s DNA matched the samples collected from the murder spot. 

During the investigation, there was a time when police considered serial killer Reinaldo Rivera to be the suspect in Jessica’s murder. Months after her murder, he was arrested. 

However, police found that Rivera’s DNA samples did not match the evidence collected. According to the Aiken Standard, Atkins told investigators about the murder weapon: a knife buried under a brush pile.

Atkins was sentenced to two consecutive life sentences on May 23, 2006, for five charges, including criminal s*xual conduct in the first degree and murder.

Later, he shared a statement in which he apologized to Jessica’s family. He is serving two consecutive life sentences in Perry County jail, and he will have no parole.
