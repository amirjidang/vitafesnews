Posted by Tandra Barner on Monday, August 19, 2024

Highlights

  • The Olsen Twins prefer privacy & fashion to stardom, focusing on their clothing line, not their childhood.
  • Elizabeth Olsen hints at positive family relationships, praising sisters for wise advice.
  • Ashley Olsen revealed they took a break to explore interests before diving into their fashion careers.

In the 1990s, Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen were everywhere, from playing Michelle Tanner on Full House to starring in many direct-to-video movies. Today, the Olsen twins are living privately and running a fashion empire. Since they don't act anymore and don't seem to want to be famous, it makes sense that the Olsens don't want their children to be celebrities.

Although Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen have chosen to keep to themselves, their many fans are still curious about the way that they live their lives, and that includes wondering about their family life. In this article, we'll take a look at whether Mary-Kate and Ashley get along with their parents David and Jarnie Olsen, along with what they have said about being child actors.

Inside Mary-Kate And Ashley Olsen's Relationship With Parents David And Jarnie Olsen

Since Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen are soprivate, they haven't said anything about their family recently. Whenever they are interviewed, which is infrequently, they talk about their fashion careers and don't mention their parents.

However, based on the information available, the Olsen twins don't seem to think poorly of their parents. But, since they don't talk about them, people continue to question the relationship, especially since the twins didn't seem to like being child stars.

According to People, Jarnette and David Olsen's 1996 divorce didn't seem to negatively impact the Olsen twins. Since Jarnette was at the Hollywood Walk of Fame ceremony for the Olsen twins in 2004, it's possible to assume that she and the twins get along well. If they didn't, it's hard to imagine that they would want her there.

According to People, David said "The girls are fine" when talking about the divorce, and he never mentioned that there was any trouble when speaking publicly. Ashley explained that she and Mary-Kate were working and not concerned with the divorce. But since the twins were encouraged to keep working at such a young age, it's hard not to wonder about what the situation was really like, especially since fans have heard that the twins didn't like being famous so young.

While it's unclear how often Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen get together with their parents or how well they get along, they seem to love their sister Elizabeth Olsen. As Just Jared Jr. reported in 2021, Elizabeth spoke to TheOffCameraShow and said that her twin sisters helped her put things into perspective as they had some wise words for her. It's interesting to note that Elizabeth mentioned their father David Olsen in this interview.

Elizabeth said:

“I think there are two things, something my sisters always say which might have come from my father at one point… it’s that ‘no’ is a full sentence. I really like that, especially as a woman. You can just say ‘no.’ I just really like that in all aspects of life."

Since Elizabeth said something positive about their father, it's possible to assume she has had a pretty good relationship with him. Like her twin sisters, she doesn't talk much about her parents in interviews. Whenever Elizabeth mentions her family, she only has praise for her siblings.

In a 2023 interview with The Financial Times, Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen talked about The Row, their impressive clothing line that they have been running since 2006. In the interview, the twins made it clear that they have put a lot of effort into their careers. They didn't mention their parents, and it's clear that they have done everything on their own.

Things got tough for the Olsen twins as soon as they turned 18.

Ashley said, “I mean, at 18, we decided to go to school and see what the world had to offer, and we started a company together in 2006, and have been in business for almost 20 years. I think everyone knows that this industry is extremely, extremely difficult. And we started with just the two of us… One step at a time. And MK and I have been running this business."

In The Financial Times interview, Mary-Kate also shared why they don't like to talk about themselves much. She said:

“We’re just private people. We’ve done interviews our whole life and, no offence, but I don’t know how this conversation is going to go. So after years of that, you just choose to stop talking to people if it’s not going to translate.”

What Did Mary-Kate And Ashley Olsen Say About Being Child Stars?

Whenever Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen reflect on their childhood, they don't sound happy about it. Since they stopped acting when they got older, it's clear that they wanted to work in fashion instead of pursuing more work in Hollywood.

While Mary-Kate has commented on the tough childhood that she and Ashley experienced, Elizabeth Olsen told Interview Magazine that from her perspective, their family is pretty regular. She said, "I just think my family is so normal, but no one wants to accept that. I find my family to be normal because there’s an understanding of what every job entails. And it is a job."

In their 2021 interview with I-D Magazine, which was a big deal since the twins hadn't talked to the media in years, Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen shared that they wanted to change careers and get away from child stardom. Ashley said:

“We had just moved to New York. We were 18 years old and I think what we did know was that we wanted to take that time to take a break from what we were previously doing and to explore things that interested us, and explore what life has to offer…”

According to The Independent, Elizabeth Olsen opened up about her sisters to Jessie Ware on the podcast Table Manners. She said:

“It’s weird growing up with that. The paparazzi part was the strange part because they follow you, and as a child it was very scary. They were obsessed with them turning 18.”
