Who is Andrew Tate Father, Emory Tate? Where Is He Now?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Sunday, August 18, 2024

Andrew Tate father, Emory Andrew Tate Jr. was an American chess player. 

Emory Tate was the father of Andrew Tate, a well-known American kickboxer and social media influencer. Emory was highly respected in the chess community, with Maurice Ashley, the first black grandmaster, describing him as “absolutely a trailblazer for African-American chess.”

Andrew inherited his father’s talent and passion for chess and was determined to make a name for himself in the game at a young age.

In addition to his successful career as a kickboxer, Andrew is also known for his intelligence and debating skills. His father’s influence and guidance were instrumental in Andrew’s development as a chess player and overall success in life.

Many may be wondering if Emory Tate was a grandmaster in chess. It is important to note that the grandmaster is the highest rank that a chess player can achieve, and is granted by the International Chess Federation (FIDE) for life.

While Emory Tate did not attain the rank of grandmaster, he was highly skilled and accomplished in the game.

According to sources, Emory was an international master, just one rank below the grandmaster. Despite not reaching the highest rank, Emory had a successful career in chess, winning the Armed Forces Chess Championship 5 times and the Indiana State Championship 6 times.

In addition to his achievements in chess, Emory also led a noteworthy and interesting life.

The relationship between Andrew Tate & his father

Andrew Tate had a close and loving relationship with his father, Emory Tate. Andrew shared many details about his father’s past in social media posts, including how his parents met and why Emory worked for American intelligence.

Emory’s intelligence and fluency in multiple languages led to a career in the government, where he met his wife Eileen Ashleigh. In addition to Andrew, Emory had two other children: Tristan and Janine.

However, Emory spent much of his life away from his children, as Eileen returned to London after the couple separated. Tragically, Emory passed away at the age of 56 while participating in a chess tournament on October 17, 2015.

Emory Tate was a remarkable and influential figure in the world of chess. Despite not achieving the rank of grandmaster, he was highly skilled and accomplished in the game, winning multiple championships and earning the title of international master.

In addition to his chess career, Emory also had a rich and varied life, with experiences that shaped and influenced his children, including Andrew Tate, who followed in his father’s footsteps as a successful and talented chess player.

Emory’s legacy lives on through his children and the impact he had on the chess community.
