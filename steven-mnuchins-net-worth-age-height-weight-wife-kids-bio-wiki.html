Early Life

Steven Terner Mnuchin was born to Elaine Terner Cooper and Robert E. Mnuchin in New York City, New York on December 21, 1962. He was the second-youngest child in the family.

He completed his schooling from Riverdale Country School located in New York City, and for his college, Mnuchin attended Yale University. In college, he was the publisher of ‘Yale Daily News.’

Personal Life

Steven Mnuchin has been married three times. From 1992 to 1999, he was married to Kathryn Leigh McCarver. He then got married to Heather deForest Crosby in 1999. The couple divorced in 2014. With her, he has three children named Emma Mnuchin, John Mnuchin, and Dylan Mnuchin. In June 2017, he got married to actress Louise Linton.

He serves in the board member of Riverdale Country School and is in Yale University’s development board.

Career

In 1985, Steven Mnuchin began working for Goldman Sachs. He first started with the mortgage department and went on to become a partner in 1994. During his career with Goldman, he was the head of the Mortgage Securities Department, Member of the Executive Committee, Chief Information Officer, and Executive Vice President. He left the company in 2002.

In 2002, Mnuchin became the vice-president of ESL Investments. He then co-founded the company called ‘SFM Capital Management.’ He is also the founder of the hedge fund Dune Capital Management.

In 2009, he and his group bought IndyMac. He then became the chairman and CEO of IndyMac, which was later renamed as OneWest. In 2015, he sold OneWest for $3.4 billion to CIT Group.

Mnuchin is the founder of RatPac-Dune Entertainment, which has produced many notable films including ‘Avatar’ and ‘X-Men’ film series. He has served as the executive producer of many films including ‘The Lego Movie’, ‘Annabelle’, ‘American Sniper’, ‘Winter’s Tale’, ‘Mad Max: Fury Road’, ‘In the Heart of the Sea’, ‘Lights Out’, ‘Suicide Squad’, and ‘Wonder Woman’.

In 2016, he was announced as the Secretary of the Treasury by President-elect Donald Trump.
